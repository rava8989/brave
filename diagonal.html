1/25 DTE Put Diagonal — Bullish Skewed
Setup — enter any time in the 12:30–15:00 ET window, exit at the same clock time the next trading day (Entry Time === Exit Time). Short a near-term put slightly in the money and buy a longer-dated put below it. Default sizing: short 10 pts ITM, long 20 pts below that (roughly 10 pts OTM). Example: SPX 6300 → sell 1 DTE 6310 put, buy ~25 DTE 6290 put.
Holding — carry the position overnight. Next session at the same clock time, exit the whole structure and simultaneously open the next day's diagonal. The 24h hold means one diagonal in the book at any given moment — no overlap, no stacked gamma.
Closing — held exactly 24 hours so the expiring short bleeds a full day of theta with no overnight-gap overlap between positions. Profit comes from the diagonal value widening because the short decays faster than the long; the daily roll is housekeeping.

⏰ Recommended entry/exit window — any intraday time between 12:30 ET and 15:00 ET, as long as Entry Time === Exit Time. This guarantees that yesterday's diagonal closes at the exact moment today's opens, so only one diagonal is live in the book at any given moment. Earlier (before 12:30 ET) picks up too much of the morning open's noise; later (after 15:00 ET) leaves too little room on the expiring-short side.

★ Canonical default config (loaded below): entry/exit anywhere in 12:30–15:00 ET (same clock time), sDTE=1, +10 ITM, lDTE=25, −20 below, filtered with OPEX-1 + NM + VIX 50–80% + AAPL + MSFT + TSLA + META in EXCLUDE mode. Conservative sizing (10 ITM / 20 wide) caps overnight-gap tail risk at ≈ debit + width ≈ $2–3K/contract. The per-ticker earnings subset (four mega-caps) targets the overnight-gap days that matter, without penalising the historically neutral GOOGL/NVDA/AMZN report days that ALL EARNINGS used to sweep up.

🧪 Filter stack — OPEX-1 (day before monthly OPEX — weakest-edge day of the cycle), NM (first trading day of month — gap risk, NM Straddle day), VIX 50–80% (uncomfortable-middle regime; avg −$117/trade there vs +$220 elsewhere), and AAPL / MSFT / TSLA / META earnings days (the four mega-caps whose after-hours prints drive the largest overnight SPX gaps). All seven active by default in EXCLUDE mode. EOM and ALL EARNINGS are intentionally OFF — the per-ticker subset is tighter.

⚠ Backtester scope — what this tool does / does NOT model:
Does: open a fresh diagonal at the selected entry time (default 12:30 ET, any time in the 12:30–15:00 ET window is valid), close it at the same clock time the next trading day (Entry Time === Exit Time), price both legs from real Polygon NBBO bid/ask snapshots. Data coverage: 2023‑01‑03 → 2026‑04‑22, 820 trading days, 20 selectable intraday buckets (09:45 → 15:45), SPX puts ±300 pts around spot, DTE 0–35 continuous (100% real quote coverage on the default config, 0% Black‑Scholes fallback). Expired short legs settle to exact intrinsic (SPX is PM‑settled at SPX close), not Black‑Scholes.
Does NOT: no rolling, no strike adjustments, no mid‑trade management, no theta/delta hedging, no slippage past mid, no commissions — every day opens a brand‑new position and closes it flat. Sizing is fixed at 1 contract by default; equity compounding is only engaged if you flip off Ignore margin in the sidebar. Live execution with rolls can and will differ from these numbers.

What do these filters mean?
Mode: pick EXCLUDE to skip those days, or ONLY to trade only those days. Then click any pill(s) to activate.

Calendar filters
  • OPEX — monthly options expiration (3rd Friday). OPEX-1 = day before, OPEX+1 = day after.
  • EOM — last trading day of the month. EOM-1 = second-to-last.
  • NM — first trading day of a new month.
  • FED — FOMC rate-decision announcement days (typically Wednesday).
  • CPI — monthly inflation report release (BLS, typically 8:30 AM ET).
  • VIX EXP — monthly VIX futures/options expiration (Wednesday 2 days before OPEX).
  • AAPL / GOOGL / MSFT / TSLA / META / NVDA / AMZN — single-ticker earnings report days (usually after-hours announcements).
  • ALL EARNINGS — the union of all 7 Mag-7 earnings days above.

VIX regime filters (data-derived, not calendar-based)
  • VIX 50–80% — the "VIX 20-day percentile" filter. Each day, we rank today's VIX against the last 20 trading days' VIX values (0% = lowest in 20 days, 100% = highest). This pill flags days where that rank lands between 50 and 80 — the "uncomfortable middle". Not cheap enough to be complacent, not elevated enough to be panic — just ambiguous. The diagonal has no edge here: in the backtest these days average −$117/trade while the rest of the distribution averages +$220/trade.
  • VIX DEAD — a tighter subset of VIX 50–80%. Flags a day only if both conditions hold:
    1. VIX ranks 50–80% vs the prior 20 trading days (the "uncomfortable middle" above), AND
    2. Yesterday's SPX moved less than 0.5% in either direction (a "flat / sleepy" prior session).
    This isolates the pure no-edge regime: elevated-but-not-spiky VIX plus no directional follow-through from the prior day. In the backtest, skipping these ~100 days (out of 800+) increases total P/L by ~$26K and cuts the max drawdown by ~26%. This is the single most powerful filter we've found.

Why a "percentile" instead of a fixed VIX level? VIX=18 means something very different in a calm month vs. a panic month. A relative rank adapts to whatever regime the market is currently in — VIX=18 can be 95th-percentile in one environment and 5th-percentile in another. The edges (complacent or panicked, relative to recent norm) are profitable; the ambiguous middle is where the strategy has no advantage.
