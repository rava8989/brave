Earnings Play v2.1 — Complete Methodology

Overnight long-stock earnings reaction system · three hard gates, LONG or PASS · ← back to the board

1 · Hypothesis

Informed traders express earnings views in short-dated options in the days before the report, and that activity carries predictive content for the announcement reaction (Pan & Poteshman 2006; Roll, Schwartz & Subrahmanyam 2010; Johnson & So 2012). We hypothesize that this content is conditionally exploitable as a 24-hour long-stock position through the print — but only when three independent conditions hold simultaneously: bullish flow in a moderate (non-crowded) band, expectations not already extended, and a name whose own history shows positive reactions get paid. Each gate removes a distinct, known failure mode; the strategy's identity is the conjunction, not any single signal.

The system trades the overnight reaction only — not post-earnings drift (Bernard & Thomas 1989), which operates on a different horizon and is out of scope.

2 · Signal construction

2.1 Flow window and hygiene

  • Only prints on contracts with ≤ 14 DTE at print time. Longer-dated (LEAP/swing) flow is bucketed separately with zero weight — it speaks to a different horizon.
  • Directional classification: BULL = aggressive call buying; BEAR = aggressive put buying. With aggressor-tagged data (CheddarFlow sweeps/blocks) this is direct; with the Schwab proxy (§3.2) it is inferred (opened premium), a stated approximation.
  • Recency weighting: the final 48 hours before the print carry the heaviest weight.
  • Multi-pass collection, union-deduplicated before scoring.

2.2 PW ratio (Gate 1 statistic)

PW = Σ premium(BULL call prints, ≤14 DTE, recency-weighted) ÷ Σ premium(BEAR put prints, ≤14 DTE, recency-weighted) Deep-ITM stock replacement: call prints with strike < 0.92 · S → tracked separately in dollars; presence STRENGTHENS, absence never kills.

2.3 Expectations measures (Gate 2)

Two-week run-up = close ÷ close₁₀ᵗʳᵃᵈⁱⁿᵍ ᵈᵃʸˢ ᵃᵍᵒ − 1; ATH proximity = close ÷ all-time-high close − 1. Both from split-adjusted daily history.

2.4 Base rate (Gate 3 statistic)

reaction(q) = next regular-session OPEN after print ÷ last CLOSE before print − 1 base_rate = frequency of reaction(q) > 0 over the last 4–8 reports (open→open where the position exits at the open; both recorded)

The exit is "sell into the morning move", so the tradable reaction is measured to the open, not the close — measuring to the close would smuggle in intraday drift the system never holds for.

3.1 Intraday scoring cadence

Gates 2 and 3 are frozen for the session (their inputs don't change intraday). Gate 1 rescoring runs every 30 minutes during market hours: cumulative session option volume updates the opened-premium estimate provisionally; the estimate is finalized overnight when OI confirms which volume actually opened.

3.2 The Schwab proxy and its honest limits

EOD/intraday chain data has no aggressor side. The proxy classifies opened premium (volume confirmed by next-morning ΔOI > 0) and assumes short-dated OTM call opening into a print is predominantly buyer-initiated. That assumption is tested, not trusted: the gate backtest (§5) runs entirely on the proxy, so live inputs match validated inputs. Where CheddarFlow tags are available they take precedence.

Verdict: LONG only if all three pass. Two of three = PASS. No overrides. "But the flow is huge" at an all-time high is the trap case by construction, not an exception candidate. Verdicts are binary — no sizing by conviction.

4.1 Execution and risk

  • Enter long stock near the final minutes before the close on report day (AMC) or the prior close (BMO).
  • Size: 3–5% of account per name. Maximum two concurrent names. Overnight binary risk is a coin flip with an edge — it is never conviction-sized.
  • Exit: sell into the next morning's move, green or red. The trade is 24 hours by definition; holding a loser "to come back" converts a defined bet into an undefined one.

5 · Validation protocol

  • Historical gate test: all three gates computed as-of each of ~3,000 past earnings events (2022-07 →) using only pre-print data; LONG-verdict events' open-reaction distribution compared against the PASS distribution and against unconditional. The 2×–5× band and gate conjunction must discriminate out-of-sample or the scanner does not go live.
  • The log is mandatory: every scored name — trades and passes — with date, ratios, deep-ITM dollars, run-up, base rate, verdict, 24h outcome, P/L. Pass outcomes are the control group that keeps the gates honest over time.
  • Costs: slippage on both legs; the morning exit is marketable into liquidity.
  • Universe note: validation events come from the 211-name activity-screened universe; the live board applies the same liquidity screen to whoever reports, so construction matches.

6 · Known limitations

  • Proxy flow lacks true aggressor side (§3.2); written-call campaigns can masquerade as bullish opened premium. Mitigated by the band's upper cut and by optional tagged data.
  • Calendar errors (date or AMC/BMO flag) are a real operational risk; the board displays its source timestamp, and a name without a confirmable report time is not traded.
  • Halts, extreme gaps, and borrow constraints are not modeled; long-stock-only keeps the worst tail (short squeeze) out of the book.
  • Small-N base rates (4–8 observations) are noisy by construction — treated as a veto gate, never as a standalone signal.
