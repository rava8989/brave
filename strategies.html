M8BF

Call butterfly · follows a signal

A 0DTE call butterfly that copies the first non-banned flow signal inside its day-of-week window and holds to the close.

WHEN IT FIRES

  • Day-of-week window: Mon 11:00–11:30, Tue 13:30–14:00, Wed 12:00–12:30, Thu 11:00–11:30 (2nd Thu → 13:30–14:00), Fri 13:00–13:30 ET
  • Takes the first non-banned flow signal posted in that window
  • Center skipped if center%100 ∈ {10,25,35,40,65,80}, plus combo bans (t1%100 → 0→95, 20→15, 55→50, 65→60, 85→90)
  • Prior-day WR ≥ 90% forces it through a block; WR = 0% forces a Straddle next day instead

BLOCKED WHEN

  • EOM & EOM-1 (last two days of month)
  • Day before OPEX
  • VIX-expiry day after OPEX
  • Single-name (non-AMZN/TSLA) earnings day
  • New-month non-Monday → converts to a Straddle
  • CPI day (hard block, never overridden)

THE BET

SPX drifts toward the center strike by the 4 PM close → max payoff. Runs past either wing or sits too far → you lose only the small debit. Risk capped at premium, reward capped at wing width − premium.

Key parameters

Per-DOW windows are load-bearing (OOS-validated 2026-06-10). One trade/day = first non-banned signal. Strikes/center come from the flow post (not computed in-house). 0DTE SPX, settled at the 4:15 close intrinsic − premium. WR overrides exclude CPI and can't cancel a firing GXBF.

Straddle

Long ATM straddle · big-move bet

Buys an ATM call + put at the open on quiet-overnight-VIX days, betting the day makes a big move in either direction.

WHEN IT FIRES

  • Small overnight VIX drop: 0 < (prior VIX close − today's VIX) ≤ 0.65 (a bigger drop belongs to GXBF)
  • Calm tape: SPX overnight gap < 0.9% AND VIX open-to-open ≤ 1.4
  • Enters ~9:32 AM as a long ATM call+put (strike = SPX open snapped to 5)
  • EOM forces an "EOM Straddle"; new-month non-Monday forces an "NM Straddle"

BLOCKED WHEN

  • CPI day
  • OPEX day
  • Regular Wednesday (unless Fed day, EOM, or NM)
  • VIX rose overnight, or dropped > 0.65
  • SPX gap ≥ 0.9%
  • VIX open-to-open > 1.4

THE BET

SPX travels far from the strike by 4 PM in either direction → the in-the-money leg beats the combined premium. A quiet day that closes near the strike → both legs decay and you lose the debit (capped ~$3,200, or ~$3,500 on EOM).

Key parameters

Debit caps: regular/NM $32, EOM $35. If entry mid ≤ cap → fill now; else rest a working limit at the cap until the 13:30 ET cutoff, then expire (no trade). Held to the 4 PM cash close; P/L = (|SPX close − strike| − debit) × 100.

GXBF

Gamma-center call butterfly

A 0DTE call butterfly pinned on the live volume-weighted dealer-gamma peak, fired ~9:36 AM on big overnight-VIX-drop days.

WHEN IT FIRES

  • Overnight VIX drop > 0.65 pts — or it's the day after OPEX
  • Fires once ~9:36 AM (window opens 9:35 so the gamma chain can settle)
  • Center = the volume-weighted dealer-gamma peak, read live from the 0DTE call chain
  • Wing W chosen as the widest 5–100 where debit > 0 and risk ≤ reward
  • Independent — fires regardless of M8BF/Straddle; allowed on CPI days

BLOCKED WHEN

  • VIX open ≥ 25
  • Last trading day of month (EOM)
  • OPEX+1 where VIX gapped up ≥ 2% overnight
  • No >0.65 VIX drop and not OPEX+1 (trigger just never fires)

THE BET

SPX drifts toward and pins the dealer-gamma strike K by the close → max payoff at exactly K. A clean trend day away from the pin (closes outside K ± W) → you lose the net debit. Risk capped at ≤ W/2 per contract (~$5,000 max).

Key parameters

Center = argmax(γ × volume × S² × 100 × 0.01) over strikes ±5% of spot, snapped to 5 (always volume-weighted since 2026-06-10). BS gamma R=0.043, Q=0.013. Wing ∈ {5..100} step 5, widest with debit ≤ W/2. Hold to 4 PM cash close (1 PM half-days). Block: VIX_MAX_GXBF = 25.

BOBF

Broken-wing call butterfly · above market

A 0DTE call butterfly placed above the market that profits if SPX grinds up into a target zone by the close — only on up-trending mornings.

WHEN IT FIRES

  • Window: first qualifying minute 10:29–12:15 ET, one trade/day
  • Type: Fri = "Friday RSI"; Mon–Thu = VIX-up or VIX-down by overnight VIX move
  • Must be up-trending: SPX above its 5-day SMA and up from the open
  • RSI(14) gate (Fri 40–65, VIX-down ≤70), VIX ≤ 23

BLOCKED WHEN

  • CPI day, OPEX, OPEX-1
  • New-month Monday, VIX expiry
  • EOM-1 & EOM-2
  • Earnings day
  • VIX > 23, or flat overnight VIX (Mon–Thu)

THE BET

SPX grinds higher into the profit zone (body ≈ spot +15–25) by the close → the structure outvalues the small debit. Stalls/drops below the lower wing, or rips far past the upper → you lose the debit. Broken-wing keeps max loss = the net debit.

Key parameters

Body = round-to-5 of spot +15 (Friday) / +25 (VIX types); wings symmetric ±30 from body. Move-from-open: Fri ≥0.10%, VIX-up ≥0.20%, VIX-down 0.20–0.70%. VIX cap 23 (rechecked live). Friday-only premium cap $12. Held to the 4 PM SPX close.

Diagonal

1/25 put diagonal · ~24h hold

Sells a near-term slightly-ITM put and buys a longer-dated put just below it, held ~24 hours — profiting as the short decays faster than the long.

WHEN IT FIRES

  • Weekday entry anchored ~12:30 ET; exits same clock time next day (one diagonal live at a time)
  • VIX 20-day percentile ≤ 50 (calm) or > 80 (panic)
  • COR1M ≥ 10
  • All four calendar filters clear

BLOCKED WHEN

  • OPEX-1, EOM, EOM-1, NM (new month)
  • COR1M < 10 (decorrelated, whippy tape)
  • VIX percentile in the (50, 80] dead-zone
  • Missing VIX-percentile or COR1M data

THE BET

Calm/orderly overnight → the near-term short put bleeds a full day of theta faster than the long, so you buy the spread back for more than you paid. A hard gap down through both strikes inverts it — worst-case loss ≈ debit + width (~$6.6k typical, up to ~$14.5k on a 1987/2020-class tail).

Key parameters

Short put +10 ITM (1 DTE), long put −20 below short (~10 OTM, width 20), long ≈ 25 calendar DTE (±5). Window 12:30–15:00 ET, entry time = exit time, ~24h hold. VIX dead-zone (50, 80]; COR1M floor 10. Filter priority OPEX-1 > EOM > EOM-1 > NM > COR1M_LOW > VIX_MID. Retuned 2026-06-09.

Tail Hedge

Long put · crash insurance

A long OTM 0DTE put bought as crash insurance when stock correlation (COR1M) crashes below 7.75 and vol-of-vol (VVIX) is calm. Bleeds small, pays big in a spike.

WHEN IT FIRES

  • COR1M crosses from above 7.75 down to ≤ 7.75 — arms "buying mode"
  • Then buy a put every morning at 9:45 ET until the first profitable day, then wait for the next cross
  • Strike = Black-Scholes delta nearest −0.10 (the "Balanced" preset)

BLOCKED WHEN

  • VVIX ≥ 110 at the open (puts too rich) — skip, stay armed
  • COR1M hasn't crossed below 7.75 (no trigger)
  • No valid strike within ±0.10 of −0.10 delta
  • Missing intraday VIX / option snapshot

THE BET

Cheap insurance during complacent, low-correlation regimes that historically precede correlated selloffs. Most days the put expires worthless (lose ~$100–500; worst single day −$320). When a vol spike hits while you hold, one day can print $5k–$15k. Many small bleeds funded by rare large convex payoffs.

Key parameters

Trigger COR1M cross-down through 7.75 (prev ≥ 7.75 AND now ≤ 7.75). VVIX skip ≥ 110. Delta target −0.10 (Balanced), ±0.10 tolerance. Entry 9:45 ET, 0DTE SPXW put, 1 contract, σ = VIX-at-entry/100. Hold to 4 PM close (1 PM half-days). 4-yr backtest ≈ +$47k over ~68 trades — low win rate, high MAR.
