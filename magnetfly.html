The rules (all must hold at 12:00 ET)

1. Alignment: the M8BF fly center sits exactly 5 pts from the day's magnet — i.e. the service's T1 is the magnet. (T1 is always center ±5; verified on all 4,309 archived signals.)
2. Calendar clear: not EOM, not CPI day, not FED day, not Mon–Thu of monthly OPEX week. OPEX Friday itself is traded (7 of 8 won).
3. Price: the 30-wide CALL fly at the magnet (K−30 / K / K+30) costs ≤ $17.00 — typical fill ~$12.30. Costlier day = skip.
4. Exit: OCO bracket — take profit +3.0 pts (+$300/lot), stop −5.0 pts (−$500/lot), hold to 4:15 settle if neither fills (never happened in 87 backtest trades).

Why it works

The magnet is the strike with the largest positive dealer-gamma (OI basis, 10:30 snapshot) — where hedging flow pins price. The Discord M8BF service independently publishes a fly whose T1 target expresses its pin estimate. On ~35% of days they agree to the strike; on those days a fly bought ON the magnet at midday grinds up as the pin holds, and a +3 pop fills 4 times out of 5. Proof it's the alignment and not "midday flies are good": the identical trade on non-aligned days loses money in every period tested (H2 far-control: −$186/lot, 31% WR). The cheap-debit rule works because a cheap fly on an aligned day means vol is priced low while the pin is already holding.
