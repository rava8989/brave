TAIL HEDGE — BALANCED (DEFAULT)
The strategy: Buy a cheap 0DTE SPX put at 9:45 AM ET whenever stock correlation (COR1M) crashes below 7.75 — but only if VVIX is under 110. Hold to 4 PM close. Most days you lose $100-500 (the put expires worthless). A few days print $5k-$15k when the market shocks. Over 4 years: +$47,040 across 68 trades, worst single loss only −$320.
The trigger (catches EVERY cross): Watch COR1M's hourly bars. The moment it transitions from above 7.75 to at-or-below 7.75 — overnight OR intraday — you're in buying mode. If the cross is on the 9:30 bar → trade same day at 9:45. If it's at 10:30 or later → trade next trading day. Keep buying every morning until the first profitable day, then back to waiting for the next cross.
The page: All numbers are NO-LOOK-AHEAD (uses only 9:30 opens) with conservative fills (entry mid rounded UP to $0.10, P/L floored to $10 for commissions + slippage). Pick any preset above to compare — Balanced is the default and recommended baseline.
R1 · Max Complacency
Low correlation + steep contango. Sunny day — stocks doing their own thing, market expects future fear. Article says "buy umbrella now," but R1 days bled $-5k in our test.
R2 · Early Warning
Low correlation but the front of the curve (9-day VIX vs 30-day VIX) is inverting. First crack appears. Very rare — only 1 day in 3 years.
R3 · Regime Turn
Correlation is rising and contango is compressing toward flat. Storm forming. Risk-off ramping. ~40 days, mixed P/L in our test.
R4 · Post-Shock
Correlation high + backwardation (today scarier than future). Storm hit. This is where the puts pay. 100% WR in our basic test, ~67% in the sweet-spot preset.
R0 · Neutral
Anything not matching the above. Most days (~80%) live here. Boring market.
