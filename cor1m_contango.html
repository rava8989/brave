TAIL HEDGE — COR1M × VIX TERM STRUCTURE
What it does: Buy a cheap 0DTE SPX put at 9:45 AM ET on days when stock correlation (COR1M) crashes below 9.0 and VVIX is calm (under 110). Hold to 4 PM close. Most days lose a little ($100-500). A few days print huge ($5k-$15k) when the market shocks.
The signal: At 9:30 AM check $COR1M open and $VVIX open. Trigger fires on fresh cross from above-9 to below-9. VVIX ≥ 110 blocks that day. Keep buying every day until first profitable day, then back to waiting.
The page: Pick a preset above (Champion + VVIX recommended) to see its equity curve, trade log, and per-regime performance. All numbers are NO-LOOK-AHEAD (uses 9:30 opens only) with conservative fills (entry mid rounded UP to $0.10, P/L floored to $10 for commissions + slippage).
R1 · Max Complacency
Low correlation + steep contango. Sunny day — stocks doing their own thing, market expects future fear. Article says "buy umbrella now," but R1 days bled $-5k in our test.
R2 · Early Warning
Low correlation but the front of the curve (9-day VIX vs 30-day VIX) is inverting. First crack appears. Very rare — only 1 day in 3 years.
R3 · Regime Turn
Correlation is rising and contango is compressing toward flat. Storm forming. Risk-off ramping. ~40 days, mixed P/L in our test.
R4 · Post-Shock
Correlation high + backwardation (today scarier than future). Storm hit. This is where the puts pay. 100% WR in our basic test, ~67% in the sweet-spot preset.
R0 · Neutral
Anything not matching the above. Most days (~80%) live here. Boring market.
