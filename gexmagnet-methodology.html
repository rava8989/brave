GEXMAGNET — Complete Methodology

Dealer-gamma level-reversion swing system for single stocks · v1 · ← back to the report

1 · Hypothesis

When option dealers are net long gamma in a stock, their delta-hedging is counter-directional: they sell into rallies and buy into declines, damping volatility and pulling price toward the strike where their gamma (and therefore hedging intensity) is greatest. We hypothesize that a confirmed upward cross of the zero-gamma level ("flip") in a positive-gamma name initiates a drift toward the maximum-positive-GEX strike ("magnet") that is exploitable on a 1–7 day horizon in names where dealer hedging is large relative to the stock's ordinary trading volume.

The damping/pinning behavior of hedging flow is documented in the academic literature (Ni, Pearson & Poteshman 2005; Barbon & Buraschi 2021; Baltussen et al. 2021) and popularized by practitioner GEX modeling (SqueezeMetrics 2017). What is NOT established is whether a tradable, cost-surviving edge exists at the single-stock level on this horizon — that is precisely what Phase 1 tests, and a NO verdict is an acceptable outcome.

2 · Gamma exposure model

2.1 Per-contract dollar gamma

GEX_contract = Γ · OI · M · S² · 0.01 [$ per 1% spot move] Γ = Black-Scholes-Merton gamma of the contract (per share) OI = open interest (contracts) M = contract multiplier (100) S = underlying spot price

The S²·0.01 term converts share-gamma into the dollar hedging flow induced by a 1% move: delta change per 1% = Γ·(0.01·S) shares per contract, valued at S dollars each.

2.2 Dealer sign convention (baseline)

Calls contribute positive GEX, puts negative — the standard assumption that customers net-buy puts and net-sell calls, leaving dealers long calls / short puts. This is known to be unreliable for single names; two mitigations are built in:

  • Day-over-day ΔGEX at key strikes is computed as a first-class output — the change in the estimate is more stable to the sign assumption than its level.
  • Phase 2 replaces the blanket assumption with per-strike dealer signs inferred from aggressor-tagged order flow joined to next-day OI changes (§7).

3.1 OI timing convention

OI published on morning T reflects positions through T−1's close. A snapshot taken after T's close therefore pairs T's quotes/IV with OI as of T−1 — the standard 1-day OI lag every EOD GEX model carries. Day-over-day OI differences between consecutive post-close snapshots attribute cleanly to single sessions.

3.2 Universe construction (outcome-blind)

12,712 option roots → stock gate (close > $5, ADV$₂₀ > $50M) → 2,028 → ranked by 10-day option premium volume ÷ ADV$₂₀ with a 10k total-OI floor → top 200 + 11 flow-history force-includes = 211. The screen uses only contemporaneous activity characteristics, never returns.

Known bias: ranking on activity measured near the end of the sample favors names that became active — this inflates signal counts in early years, not per-trade quality, and the SPY control absorbs general-drift effects. The live scanner re-runs the same screen quarterly so production matches the tested construction.

4 · Trading rules (v1 — deliberately minimal)

4.1 Entry — all required

  • Day T close below flip(T); positive net GEX; flip, magnet, wall all defined.
  • Confirmed cross: first 5-min close above flip at the regular open (live). Backtest fallback (EOD data only): T+1 open > flip(T), flagged fallback_entry on every trade.
  • Reward/risk: (magnet − entry) ÷ (entry − flip) ≥ 2.0.
  • Materiality: GEX/ADV$₂₀ ≥ θmat — fitted in walk-forward, never hand-picked.
  • Market gate: SPY session gap ≥ θspy (fitted). One market gate, one vol gate maximum; no breadth gates unless they prove out net of the market gate.

4.2 Exit ladder (priority order; same-day collisions resolve worst-first)

  • Hard cap: −10% from entry while below the (current) flip → exit immediately at the cap (at the open if gapped through).
  • Structural stop: daily close below the put wall (updated daily, last-known if undefined) → exit next open.
  • T1: touch of the magnet (frozen at entry) → exit at the level, or move stop to entry and ride to the next structural level. Riding without locking the entry-stop is forbidden.
  • Time stop: day 7 with < 50% progress toward T1 → exit at close.

4.3 Instruments & sizing

Whole shares (IRA) or deep-ITM calls, delta ≥ 0.80, several weeks to expiry (margin account) sized to equivalent delta-dollar risk — never ATM/OTM premium; the thesis is a price-level thesis and pays no theta rent. Fixed fractional risk per trade to the structural stop; results are reported in R multiples.

5 · Validation protocol

  • SPY control: every trade spawns a shadow position — same dates, same notional, SPY instead. Every headline metric is strategy − control. This kills "it worked because everything went up."
  • Costs: 5 bps slippage per side (configurable) on strategy and control; never gross-only.
  • Walk-forward: θmat and θspy are fitted on a training window, applied unchanged to the next out-of-sample window, then rolled. Only OOS results are quoted.
  • Evidence bar: no parameter becomes a default without ≥ 100 OOS trades behind it.
  • No exception clauses: if a filter needs an exception, the filter is wrong (spec hard rule).
  • Regime disclosure: results split by calendar year; an edge that lives in one year is reported as exactly that.

6 · Known limitations

  • EOD backtest cannot express the live 5-min confirmation rule; the open-above-flip fallback is stricter in gaps and looser in intraday reversals — flagged per trade, reconciled in paper trading (two weeks of live alerts must match backtest logic before capital).
  • The baseline dealer-sign assumption is least reliable in heavily speculative names — ironically the ones the materiality screen favors. Phase 2 exists because of this.
  • Greeks are vendor BSM values; deep-ITM/near-expiry IV is noisy (nulled where non-computable).
  • Borrow, halts, and earnings gaps are not modeled; entries are not blocked around earnings in v1 — "skip earnings week" is measured as a robustness check, not assumed.

7 · Phase 2 — flow-informed dealer signs (planned)

Aggressor-tagged order flow (CheddarFlow BULL/BEAR sweeps and blocks) is joined to next-morning OI changes at the same strike/expiry to classify opened interest as customer-bought vs customer-sold, yielding per-strike dealer signs in place of the blanket convention. The Phase 1 backtest then reruns identically under flow-informed GEX; the two runs' OOS comparison is the entire deliverable, and the simpler model wins ties.
