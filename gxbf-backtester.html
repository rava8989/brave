GXBF — 0DTE SPXW Long-Call Gamma Butterfly (hybrid)
0DTE SPXW long-call butterfly centered on the morning Major-Positive gamma strike, with the center sourced from either traded volume or open interest depending on the day. The day is gated by an overnight VIX rule, the wing widens to the widest risk ≤ reward width, and the position is held to the SPX cash close.

Trigger (one of must be true):
  • overnight VIX drop > 0.65 pts (prev VIX close − today VIX open), OR
  • it's OPEX+1 (the trading day after monthly options expiration — auto-trigger).
Block (any one kills the trade):
  • today's VIX open ≥ 25 (hard volatility cap),
  • OPEX+1 and VIX gapped up ≥ 2% overnight,
  • it's EOM (last trading day of the month — handed to Straddle).
Allowed days — OPEX-1, CPI, and NM-non-Monday are no longer banned; they fire if the trigger/block check passes. GXBF is fully independent of M8BF / Straddle / BOBF — it neither blocks nor is blocked by them.

Center routing (hybrid, live default) — at the chosen Entry Time (default 09:35 ET; the dropdown below offers every 5-min step from 09:35 through 14:00) the gamma model computes two centers from the 0DTE call chain (Black-Scholes γ within ±5% of spot, snapped to the nearest 5):
  • Volume center — γ × totalVolume peak (captures today's traded flow).
  • OI center — γ × openInterest peak (captures standing dealer inventory).
Hybrid routes the day automatically: on OPEX-1, VIX expiry (Wed two days before OPEX), or FED announcement days → use the OI center; on all other days → use the Volume center.

Structure — symmetric long-call butterfly: buy 1 lower call (K − W), sell 2 center calls K, buy 1 upper call (K + W) for a net debit nd = lower − 2·center + upper.
Wing selection — sweep W ∈ {5, 10, … 100} (5-pt steps); a width is valid when nd > 0 AND nd ≤ W − nd (i.e. nd ≤ W/2, max risk never exceeds max reward). Take the widest valid W; if none qualify, no trade. Per-contract risk is the debit paid (capped near $5,000 via the W ≤ 100 + risk ≤ reward constraint).
Holding / closing — 0DTE SPXW, held to the SPX cash close (16:00 ET; 13:00 ET on half-days). Settled to exact intrinsic clamp(max(0,S−(K−W)) − 2·max(0,S−K) + max(0,S−(K+W)), 0, W); pnl = (intrinsic − nd) × 100 × contracts.

★ Default config: entry 09:35 ET (configurable 09:35–14:00 in 5-min steps), exit SPX close (0DTE), wing 5 → 100 (step 5), widest risk ≤ reward wing, hybrid center, real GXBF gating ON. The Entry Time dropdown studies what-if the bot fired later in the morning / midday. The other toggles can force vol-only, OI-only, or replace the live gating with the legacy manual overnight VIX-drop filter for what-if studies.

⚠ Backtester scope — opens one butterfly at the selected Entry Time ET, settles to exact SPX-close intrinsic; no rolling, no mid-trade management, no slippage past mid, no commissions. Sizing is 1 contract by default; equity compounding engages via the allocation sidebar. Special-day filters apply on the entry date. GXBF trigger / block gating is computed from full-day VIX (vixOpen / vixClose / gap) — unaffected by Entry Time. Live execution can differ.

What do these filters mean?
Mode: pick EXCLUDE to skip those days, or ONLY to trade only those days. Then click any pill(s) to activate.

Calendar filters
  • OPEX — monthly options expiration (3rd Friday). OPEX-1 = day before, OPEX+1 = day after.
  • EOM — last trading day of the month. EOM-1 = second-to-last.
  • NM — first trading day of a new month.
  • FED — FOMC rate-decision announcement days (typically Wednesday).
  • CPI — monthly inflation report release (BLS, typically 8:30 AM ET).
  • VIX EXP — monthly VIX futures/options expiration (Wednesday 2 days before OPEX).
  • AAPL / GOOGL / MSFT / TSLA / META / NVDA / AMZN — single-ticker earnings report days (usually after-hours announcements).
  • ALL EARNINGS — the union of all 7 Mag-7 earnings days above.

Overnight VIX drop is a separate gate (not a pill): enable the checkbox in the params row to trade only days where prev VIX close − today VIX open ≥ threshold (default 0.65 pts). VIX percentile-band categories are intentionally not offered for GXBF.
